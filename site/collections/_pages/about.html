Hi, I’m Isaac! My interest in environmental science started young, spending lots of time catching crabs and exploring estuaries. I’ve always seen the ocean as a vast frontier—one we still have so much to learn from. That curiosity led me to pursue a career in coastal and ocean science. I received a Bachelors in Environmental Science from Emory University and graduated from Duke University with a Masters in Coastal Environmental Management, where I worked in the Marine Robotics and Remote Sensing Lab run by Dave Johnston. Now I'm a researcher and drone pilot at NOAA’s Northeast Fisheries Science Center.

I’ve been flying drones since 2013, when they first became more widely available, and I’m fascinated by the unique perspectives they provide. There’s something incredible about seeing the world from above, and it’s even more exciting to use that vantage point to further scientific research. My work combines technology, innovation, and the drive to protect our coasts and oceans in new, meaningful ways. I look to streamline processes in my research, making it easier for others to engage in science no matter their technical skill level. I believe there’s always room for improvement, and I love finding new ways to make methods more efficient to direct focus on what matters most—conducting impactful science.

When I’m not working, I enjoy DJing for friends, experimenting in the kitchen, hiking in nature, or enjoying a round of golf or soccer. Whether it’s through my research or personal interests, I’m always chasing new experiences and ways to explore the world.
